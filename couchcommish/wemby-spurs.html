The Alamo Reawakens: The Wembanyama-Popovich Era Begins in San Antonio

Brandon Novarro | May 25, 2023 • 5 min

In one of the most highly-anticipated draft lotteries in years, the San Antonio Spurs emerged as the victors, securing the first overall pick in the upcoming 2023 NBA draft. On June 22nd, the Silver and Black will hold the key to draft Victor Wembanyama, arguably the greatest NBA draft prospect than none other than Lebron James. This will mark the third time in the franchise's history that the Spurs have held the coveted first pick. What happened the other two times they picked number one, you may ask? Well, San Antonio fared pretty well, if I may say, coming away with Hall of Fame big men David Robinson in 1987 and Tim Duncan in 1997. Both of whom were key figures who ignited the Spurs' NBA record 22-year playoff streak from 1998-2019, cementing San Antonio's place in NBA history.

However, recent years have seen the Spurs struggle, a departure from their storied past. Since their last playoff appearance in 2019, they have gone 32-39, 33-39, 34-48, and 22-60, making the worst stint in the career of legendary HC Gregg Popovich. But with this lottery win, the Silver and Black are poised to jump back into relevancy and potentially bring back the glory days. The reason for this optimism?: A 7-foot-5 French prospect named Victor Wembanyama, hailed as a "generational talent" by none other than LeBron James himself.

Victor Wembanyama's name has been making rounds in basketball circles for quite some time now. His talent has left the basketball world in awe, with his putback dunk off his own 3-pointer being a testament to his unique abilities. His recent performances for his French club, Metropolitans 92, further showcase his dominance. Wembanyama led the team to a playoff berth in the LNB Pro A, clinching the 2022-23 scoring title in the process.

This young talent's impressive performance statistics tell a compelling story. He is a consistent double-double threat. His notable performances include a 29-point and 16-rebound game on April 8, leading Metropolitans 92 to a 93-80 victory over Graffenstaden. More recently, Wembanyama had a 21-point, 14-rebound performance in a playoff game against Cholet on May 23. Against the G-league team Ignite last year, Wemby scored 37 points with 7 3-pointers. Keep in mind he is 7-foot-5 with an 8-foot wingspan.

Wembanyama's impact goes beyond his scoring prowess. His presence on the court is multi-dimensional, contributing to assists, steals, and blocks as well. In the first playoff game following the NBA lottery, he contributed 22 points, 10 rebounds, 6 assists, 2 steals, and 2 blocks, aiding Met 92 in securing a 93-80 win over Cholet. As some may say, he is a unicorn, a unique do-it-all NBA prospect that is as rare as they come. Think Kevin Durant mixed with Rudy Gobert or any absurd combination that seems entirely out of this world. That’s Wemby.

The enormity of Victor's prowess is challenging to encapsulate merely through words. There is a reason he is the most hyped draft prospect since King James. The tale is in the tape.


As the Spurs look forward to welcoming this remarkable talent, it's evident that Victor Wembanyama is not just another draft pick. His unique abilities and vision for his emerging career will likely infuse new energy into the Spurs' squad.

Though, the future outlook of the Spurs with Victor Wembanyama on the team isn't entirely clear yet. Given San Antonio's successful history of big men, Wembanyama could well be the next big thing for the Spurs and the league. However, a question that looms larger is how the Spurs plan to structure their team around this potential new linchpin.

While it's hoped that future building blocks may already exist in the forms of forward Keldon Johnson (23 years old, averaging 22 points per game), guard Devin Vassell (22 years old, averaging 18.5 points per game), and forward Jeremy Sochan (20 years old), the current roster overall lacks a distinctive identity. The aspiration is to sculpt a formidable team around Wembanyama, a task that could potentially be aided by the arsenal of draft picks at the Spurs' disposal. This includes as many as six first-round picks and five second-round picks in the 2024 and 2025 NBA Drafts, contingent on pick protections, acquired through recent trades involving players such as Dejounte Murray. This impressive draft capital may provide a unique opportunity for a strategic rebuild around Wembanyama's undeniable potential.

The era of Spurs basketball, defined by legends Tim Duncan, Manu Ginóbili, and Tony Parker, is long gone. However, the Spurs winning the NBA lottery could be a turning point for the franchise, offering a chance to return to their golden days. Victor Wembanyama's addition to the team has the potential to redefine the Spurs' identity and playoff fortunes. His unique skill set and incredible size bring a new dimension to the court, one that could very well shake up the Western Conference and perhaps the entire NBA. With four straight seasons missing the postseason, It looked as if 74-year-old Popovich and Spurs' reign of dominance in the NBA had ended. Not so fast. The 5-time NBA champion HC Gregg Popovich may have unfinished business in the league. As we approach the 2023 NBA Draft, the anticipation builds, and the question remains: Will Victor Wembanyama usher in a new era of Spurs dominance?
